La DANA de Valencia

October 2024, Valencia, Spain

La DANA de Valencia

October 2024, Valencia, Spain

On the 29th of October 2024, an intense rainfall causing mudslides and flooding occurred in the area of the Valencian Community where my family and friends live. More than 200 people perished. It is one of the deadliest natural disasters in the history of Spain. Thousands of volunteers from around Spain mobilized and went to help with the cleanup and recovery of the community. I decided to take a one-week leave from work and join family and friends to help in the effort to restore a friend's workplace, which was deemed a priority business since they provide working materials, boots, gloves, and other necessary items to be given to other workforces.

Medical caravan

Riobamba, Ecuador, March 2023

Medical caravan

Riobamba, Ecuador, March 2023

I went as a volunteer with a group of Princeton's students to the Andean region in Ecuador to implement the work of a team of ophthalmologists from the Conestoga Eye Group. We screened almost two thousand children for glasses and identified conditions that can be treated at the clinic we work with in Riobamba. Our team was in charge of setting the multiple stations where we performed the complete eye exams (measurements, visual acuity, dilating drops, pupil measurements, scribe, interpreters, choosing the frames). We were lucky to also interact with the children once done with the stations and exchange about our life and play soccer.
